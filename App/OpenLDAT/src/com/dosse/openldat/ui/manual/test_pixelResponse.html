Pixel Response Time measurement





Pixel Response Time is the time it takes for a pixel to transition from a color to another.
This test measures the transition times between black and white, as well as between several shades of grey.
This test takes 1-3 minutes to complete depending on the chosen settings.

The following settings can be changed:

  • Evaluation method: which range of the transition should be considered. Two options are available:
    • VESA Standard: the gold standard, considers the range between 10% and 90%. Not often used by manufacturers outside of professional monitors.
    • BS Manufacturers Say: a completely arbitrary range of 30% to 70% typically used by manufacturers, especially in the gaming industry.
  • Step size: smaller steps means that more shades of grey are tested, but the test will take longer. Possible values are 16 (slowest), 32 (balanced), 64 (fast).



PWM, Strobing and other noise

The accuracy of this test is reduced if PWM backlights are used or some other similar noise is present. If possible, it should be turned off to get better results.
